The billing model

Copilot usage-based billing combines fixed monthly seat fees with shared AI credit pools.

ConceptPlain-English meaning
Seat / licenseFixed monthly access cost ($19 Business / $39 Enterprise).
Included creditsMonthly AI credit allowance granted by each active seat.
Shared poolAll included credits combine into one bucket for the billing entity.
Metered usageOverage at $0.01 / AI credit after the pool is exhausted.
Billing cyclePool resets 00:00 UTC on the 1st; credits do not roll over.
Core principle: You pay fixed seat fees to fund a shared credit bucket. After the bucket is empty, usage becomes metered unless a budget stops it.

What costs money

Know what is included in the license fee versus what consumes AI credits.

Included Code completions and next-edit suggestions.
Metered Copilot Chat, CLI, coding/cloud agents, Spaces, Spark, and third-party agents.
Memory aid: Typing help is included; thinking/agent work is metered.

Why costs can vary

Most variation comes from model choice, context size, output length, and agent/tool work.

ModelPowerful models cost more.
ContextMore files or history uses more credits.
Work doneAgents may read files, call tools, retry, or generate longer output.
Rule of thumb: Bigger model + more context + more agent work = higher cost.

Budget controls

Two budget families. Learn the split and the rest follows.

Universal user-levelEach user's total credit useAlways (pool + metered)Always
Cost-center user-levelEach member's total use (per cost center)AlwaysAlways
Individual user-levelOne specific user's total useAlwaysAlways
Cost center budgetA cost center's metered chargesMetered phase onlyOnly if "Stop usage" is enabled

User-level budgets

Controls how much each person can use in total.

Applies from the first credit used. When the person reaches the limit, usage stops.

Group budgets

Controls how much paid overage a cost center, organization, or enterprise can spend.

Only matters after included credits are used up. It blocks only when “Stop usage” is enabled.

Important default: “Stop usage when budget limit is reached” is off by default on group budgets.
Advanced cost center options
  • Enterprise teams can be cost-center resources: membership stays current automatically as people join or leave the enterprise team.
  • Cost-center AI credit pools: limit how much of the included monthly AI credit pool a cost center can use before overage starts.
  • Per-cost-center user-level budgets: set one per-user AI credit limit that applies to all members of the cost center.

Which limit wins?

  • If several user limits apply, use the most specific one: Individual → Cost-center user-level → Universal.
  • The first limit to run out blocks usage — even if another limit still has room.
  • A $0 budget blocks usage immediately.
  • Raise the budget that was actually hit. Raising a group budget will not help someone who hit their user-level limit.

Incident runbook — users blocked or credits exhausted

Goal: restore critical developer work without opening unlimited spend.

Golden rule: Do not remove caps or turn off hard stops. Find what limit was hit, then unblock only the users or groups that need it.

1. Confirm the blocker

Find out what actually stopped usage.

  • Pool empty: included credits are used up.
  • User limit hit: one person reached their cap.
  • Group cap hit: cost center, organization, or enterprise overage cap stopped usage.

2. Communicate

Reduce panic while the fix is prepared.

  • Tell developers: completions and next-edit suggestions still work.
  • Tell managers: max exposure is controlled by the overage cap.
  • Share when the first usage readout will be available.

3. Find the usage driver

Identify whether this is normal demand or a spike.

  • Open Billing and licensing → Usage → AI usage.
  • Use Time Frame, Filter, and Group to find top users, organizations, cost centers, and models.
  • Export the usage report when you need date-level or user/model evidence.
  • Compare credits used with days elapsed in the billing month to identify a spike.
Show navigation screenshots

4. Unblock safely

Restore access without creating a blank check.

  • Raise limits only for approved power users or critical groups.
  • Keep enterprise and group caps in place with hard stops enabled.
  • Re-check sizing math before increasing broad limits.

Cost levers (share with engineering)

Real savings come from behavior and model choice, not just caps.

LeverWhy it works
Use lighter models for routine workLower per-token cost.
Keep context smallFewer input tokens; avoids long-context price tiers.
Do not dump whole repos or history into agentsBig context + big output is the most expensive pattern.
Use included coding suggestions firstInline completions and next-edit suggestions do not consume AI credits.
Keep IDEs and Copilot extensions currentUpdated tools show usage more accurately and reduce billing confusion.

Money math you'll actually use

1 AI credit= $0.01 USD
$10 budget= 1,000 AI credits
Included AI credits (Business)= seats × 1,900 credits
Included AI credits (Enterprise)= seats × 3,900 credits
Highest planned monthly bill= license fees + approved overage cap
Before using this for billing decisions: Prices and included AI credit allowances can change. Validate against current GitHub Docs and your GitHub agreement.
